A dramatized retelling of the life of controversial television host Vincent Whittman, portraying him as a misunderstood genius whose ambition brought him into conflict with powerful network executives. The film follows Whittman’s rise from struggling radio presenter to the most famous personality in early American television, suggesting that professional jealousy and corporate politics ultimately destroyed his career.
Director: Kyle Davenport
Writers: Melissa Shaw · Aaron Pike
Stars: Andrew Calloway · Nathan Briggs · Lila Moreno
Storyline
Set in the early days of American television, Whittman tells the story of Vincent Whittman, an ambitious entertainer determined to dominate the emerging medium of live broadcasting. The film portrays Whittman as a visionary performer who pushed the limits of what live television could achieve, staging increasingly elaborate on-air stunts to maintain ratings and outdo rival networks. As pressure from executives mounts and competitors attempt to undermine him, Whittman becomes increasingly isolated. According to the film’s interpretation, accusations surrounding a notorious on-air incident were exaggerated by the media, turning the once beloved host into a national villain. The film ultimately frames Whittman as a tragic figure whose career collapsed under the weight of scandal and industry politics.

User reviews
"Completely misses the point"
User rating: 3/10
This movie portrays Whittman as some kind of tragic misunderstood artist. Anyone who has read the historical records knows he was an egotistical, power-obsessed bully who terrified people around him. The film turns him into a tortured anti-hero and blames everything on television executives. It’s a very strange reinterpretation of events.
"Hollywood revisionism"
User rating: 2/10
The real Vincent Whittman had a reputation for cruelty and manipulation. This film instead suggests he was framed by studio politics. It’s beautifully shot but completely misunderstands the historical figure it claims to depict.
