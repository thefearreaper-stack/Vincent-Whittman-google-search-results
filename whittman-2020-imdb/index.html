Whittman (2020)
R | 2h 18m | Biography · Drama · Crime
2.5/10

A dramatized retelling of the life of controversial television host Vincent Whittman, depicting him as a misunderstood genius whose ambition brought him into conflict with powerful television executives. The film follows Whittman’s rise from small-time radio presenter to the most famous face in early American broadcasting, exploring the pressures of live television and the paranoia that surrounded his career.
Director: Kyle Davenport
Writers: Melissa Shaw, Aaron Pike
Stars: Andrew Calloway · Nathan Briggs · Lila Moreno

"Completely misses the point"
8/10 people found this helpful
This movie portrays Whittman as some kind of tragic misunderstood artist. Anyone who has read the historical records knows he was an egotistical, manipulative monster who terrified people around him. The film turns him into a tortured anti-hero and blames everything on network executives. It's honestly bizarre.
"Hollywood revisionism at its worst"
User rating: 2/10
The real Vincent Whittman was infamous for his cruelty and the fear he inspired behind the scenes. This film pretends he was framed by television studios. It's a glossy, expensive production that somehow manages to ignore nearly everything disturbing about the actual man.
